01 — Project Description

What Is BrainBoost?

BrainBoost is a free web platform of interactive mini-games built specifically for teens aged 13–18. Unlike passive entertainment, every game is engineered to strengthen real cognitive skills — memory, logical reasoning, reaction speed, and sustained focus.

Teens spend hours on screens but rarely in ways that build mental capability. BrainBoost turns screen time into brain time, making improvement feel like play.

04c — Sponsor Interview

What Our Sponsor Thinks

Before writing a single line of code, we interviewed real people to validate our idea. Here's what we learned — and how it shaped BrainBoost.

🎓

AkhmetABI & Alpmapys

Tutor & Classmate — Bolat Zhantore and Khakimzhan Yerniyaz

📅 07.02.2026
Q1 — The Problem
Do students actually struggle with focus and memory today?
"Many students have trouble concentrating because of constant distractions from social media and fast-paced content. This affects academic performance, confidence, and long-term learning habits. Mental skills are just as important as physical health — yet they're often ignored."
Q2 — Target Audience
Who should this product be built for?
"Primarily students and teenagers, as well as young adults who want to improve mental performance. The game should be welcoming to beginners but still challenging enough to stay interesting as users improve."
Q3 — Features
What would make this product credible and useful?
"Multiple mini-games, increasing difficulty, and a scoring system add credibility. It should be interactive, provide clear feedback, and track progress through levels. Overly complicated features are not necessary for a student project."
Q4 — Scope
What are your expectations given this is a student project?
"A functional prototype rather than a commercial product. Simple but polished design. Stable performance without major bugs. It is better to do a few things well than to try to do too much."
Q5 — Values
What would make you proud to support this project?
"A project that helps users improve focus, memory, reaction speed, and thinking skills through games rather than pressure or stress. The focus on self-improvement rather than harmful content matters a lot."
Q6 — Advice
What is your top advice before we start building?
"Focus on user experience. Keep the design simple. Make sure the game is enjoyable as well as useful. Show effort, creativity, and reflection in every part of the project."

💡 What We Learned — And How It Changed Our Design

  • 🎯
    The sponsor confirmed that teenagers are the right audience — we locked our age range to 13–18 and designed all difficulty curves around that group.
  • 📈
    Feedback on progressive difficulty led us to add three difficulty levels per game instead of a single flat challenge.
  • 🧹
    The "do a few things well" advice helped us cut two planned games from scope to polish the core six properly.
  • 📊
    The sponsor's emphasis on tracking progress directly inspired our Progress Dashboard feature with per-skill visual charts.
